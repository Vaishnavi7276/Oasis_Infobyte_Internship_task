Tribute to Savitribai Phule

"The pioneer of women's education and a true social reformer."

Who was Savitribai Phule?

Savitribai Phule (1831–1897) was a revolutionary social reformer, educationist, and poet who played a crucial role in women's education in India. She, along with her husband Jyotirao Phule, fought against social injustices, particularly in the field of caste and gender discrimination.

In an era when educating women was taboo, she courageously established the first girls' school in India in 1848. Despite facing severe opposition, she continued to empower women and oppressed communities through education.

Apart from being a teacher and activist, she was a poet whose works inspired people to rise against discrimination and strive for equality.

Biography

Born on January 3, 1831, in Maharashtra, Savitribai Phule was married at the age of nine to Jyotirao Phule. With the encouragement of her husband, she became one of the first female teachers in India and dedicated her life to educating women and marginalized communities.

She actively fought against caste oppression and worked for widow remarriage, setting up shelters for destitute women. Her relentless efforts towards social change earned her a lasting legacy in India’s history.

Achievements

  • Founded the first girls' school in India in 1848.
  • Established several schools for untouchables and marginalized communities.
  • Opened a shelter home for widows and abandoned women.
  • Fought against child marriage and the mistreatment of widows.
  • Wrote inspiring poetry advocating social justice and women's rights.
  • Led campaigns for sanitation and hygiene awareness.

Her Legacy

Savitribai Phule is remembered as the "Mother of Indian Feminism." Her tireless efforts in education and social reform have transformed countless lives. Today, numerous schools, awards, and scholarships are named in her honor to continue her mission of education and equality.

She remains an inspiration for educators, feminists, and activists fighting for equal rights worldwide.
